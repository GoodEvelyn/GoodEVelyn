Flight to Chongqing

After that phone call, I didn’t look at my phone again, nor did I contact Dylan. He probably understood that catching up before meeting again would only increase the awkwardness. If we truly had a story, it should be saved for when we meet again.

The following evening, I rushed to pack my belongings and headed out the door. I spent the entire night madly recalling his memory of “Meditations,” but to no avail. Why did I forget? How did I forget?

I have a series of scattered memories, organized in my mind as a somewhat orderly memoir, but Dylan...it’s as if he has been removed from my life, like the white veins on an orange peel that naturally fall away, or like a tiny droplet in a rushing stream of blood that quietly falls outside the body after being cut by an unintentional scrape.

Lost in thought, I arrived at the domestic departure gate of the airport, where a patient driver helped me retrieve my luggage from the trunk before bidding me farewell.

I didn’t have much with me and smoothly passed through security before boarding. Before takeoff, I sent a flight information MMS to Dylan and then shut my eyes.

May God help me remember as much as possible during these two hours.

“Meditations,” “Meditations…”

I vaguely recall something before “Meditations.”

Was it December or January? In any case, it was a season when breath turned into mist. I squatted on the roadside, but can’t remember whether I was squatting or sitting… Can’t remember.

And so, I spent only a few minutes lost in my memories, using the rest of the time to sleep.

The cabin lights shone as bright as day, casting a halo on my memories, making them even more indistinct, and causing my eyes to become sore with prolonged viewing. But I am not someone who weeps over memories.

I disembarked with batches of passengers and went to pick up my luggage. The design of Jiangbei Airport was different from that of Lishe Airport. It felt like I had truly come back home. I love this city, without reason.

As I walked out of the arrival gate, I hesitated with each step as I dragged my luggage forward. To be honest, I was not sure if Dylan would come to pick me up, just like when I went to pick him up. Giving in to my memories, I put on my headphones and tried to walk more nonchalantly, just like how he appeared in my memory. I told myself not to look around, so I wouldn’t appear as someone who was not expected. Some of the people in front of me joyfully hugged their pick-up person, while others headed straight to the car pick-up point to continue their journey. I wouldn’t stand out among them, and I just looked like a member of the second group. Suddenly, I felt relieved.

I was about to reach the exit gate, but my steps began to feel heavy. Taylor Swift’s “Exile” started playing on my headphones. I realized that I really had seen this kind of scene somewhere before, just like the lyrics said.

Then, someone lightly tapped on my shoulder, and I trembled as I turned around.

Dylan.

It wasn’t the gray-blue hair that I had expected, but at that moment, I experienced an indescribable feeling of time standing still. Everything around me seemed to disappear in a whirl, allowing my gaze to focus on the face in front of me. First the chin, then the bridge of the nose, and finally, the eyes.

Eyes are not always honest, but they never lie.

This pair of eyes in front of me concealed only one percent of the truth, and I will never forget them.

I forgot the shape of the nose they were set in, forgot the face they were embedded in, but I remembered the look in those eyes as they gazed back at me, including the look they gave me one evening a week before Christmas eight years ago when I was throwing up on the side of the road.

The world stood still for the next second, and I did my best to cut off the connection between my tear ducts and my brain. I greeted him, “Do you like Taylor Swift too?”
